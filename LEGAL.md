# Legal Considerations

## ⚠️ Important Legal Information

This tool is provided for **educational, accessibility, and research purposes only**. Please read and understand the following before using this software.

## YouTube Terms of Service

YouTube's Terms of Service generally prohibit downloading content unless explicitly permitted by the content creator or YouTube. Key points:

### What YouTube's ToS Says

From [YouTube's Terms of Service](https://www.youtube.com/t/terms):
- You may access content for streaming only
- You may not download content unless a download button or link is provided
- Circumventing technical protection measures may violate the ToS

### This Tool's Position

**youtube-transcriber-mcp** uses `yt-dlp` to download audio for transcription purposes. While the tool itself is neutral, **how you use it matters**.

## Legal & Ethical Use Cases

### ✅ Generally Acceptable Uses

1. **Your Own Content**
   - Transcribing videos you uploaded
   - Creating captions for your own content

2. **Accessibility**
   - Creating transcripts for deaf/hard of hearing individuals
   - Making content accessible to those who need text versions
   - Educational accommodations

3. **Fair Use / Fair Dealing** (jurisdiction-dependent)
   - Research and academic purposes
   - Criticism and commentary
   - Educational use in classroom settings
   - Personal study notes

4. **With Permission**
   - Content where the creator has granted permission
   - Public domain or Creative Commons licensed content
   - Content explicitly marked as downloadable

### ❌ Generally Unacceptable Uses

1. **Commercial Redistribution**
   - Republishing transcripts without permission
   - Selling transcribed content
   - Building commercial databases of transcripts

2. **Copyright Infringement**
   - Transcribing copyrighted content for redistribution
   - Using transcripts to create derivative works without permission

3. **Terms of Service Violations**
   - Bulk downloading of content
   - Automated scraping at scale
   - Circumventing paywalls or premium content

## Regional Laws

### United States
- Fair Use (17 U.S.C. § 107) may permit some uses
- DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) considerations
- ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) may support accessibility use cases

### European Union
- Copyright Directive exceptions for accessibility
- GDPR considerations if processing personal data
- Digital Single Market provisions

### Other Jurisdictions
- Laws vary significantly by country
- Check your local copyright and fair use laws
- Some countries have stronger fair dealing provisions

## Responsible Use Guidelines

### DO:
✅ Use for personal educational purposes
✅ Create accessibility features (captions, transcripts)
✅ Respect copyright and creators' rights
✅ Only transcribe content you have permission to access
✅ Give credit to original content creators
✅ Use transcripts for research and study
✅ Check if videos already have official transcripts

### DON'T:
❌ Redistribute transcripts without permission
❌ Use for commercial purposes without authorization
❌ Bulk download or scrape at scale
❌ Circumvent paywalls or premium content
❌ Violate YouTube's Terms of Service
❌ Claim transcripts as your own work
❌ Use to create competing content

## Recommendations

### Before Using This Tool

1. **Read YouTube's ToS**: [https://www.youtube.com/t/terms](https://www.youtube.com/t/terms)
2. **Check Copyright Status**: Is the content copyrighted?
3. **Verify Permission**: Do you have permission to download?
4. **Consider Alternatives**: Does the video already have transcripts?
5. **Assess Your Use Case**: Is your use educational, accessible, or fair use?

### Best Practices

- **Start Small**: Don't bulk download
- **Keep Private**: Don't redistribute transcripts
- **Give Credit**: Always cite original sources

- **Ask Permission**: When in doubt, ask the content creator
